# Customer Service Artifacts

## Domain
Support flows, tickets, live chat, escalation, context visibility, and service quality

## Artifact Inventory

### 1. Support Journey Map
- Purpose:
  define the customer path from FAQ to chatbot to human support to resolution
- Owner:
  support owner
- Format:
  journey document
- Related sprint:
  Sprint 5
- Related modules:
  Support, Chatbot, Orders, Notifications
- Completion criteria:
  handoffs and resolution states are visible and orderly

### 2. Ticket Lifecycle Artifact
- Purpose:
  define ticket states, ownership transfer, prioritization, and closure rules
- Owner:
  support owner
- Format:
  lifecycle document
- Related sprint:
  Sprint 5
- Related modules:
  Support, Admin
- Completion criteria:
  ticket progression is explicit from creation to closure

### 3. Agent Context Visibility Rules
- Purpose:
  define what account, order, and payment context an agent may see while resolving a case
- Owner:
  support owner with security reviewer
- Format:
  visibility policy
- Related sprint:
  Sprint 5 and Sprint 6
- Related modules:
  Support, Accounts, Orders, Payments
- Completion criteria:
  agents have enough context to help without overexposure

### 4. Service Level And Escalation Matrix
- Purpose:
  define service expectations by issue severity and customer urgency
- Owner:
  support owner
- Format:
  SLA matrix
- Related sprint:
  Sprint 5
- Related modules:
  Support, Admin, Notifications
- Completion criteria:
  response expectations are measurable and aligned with issue criticality

## Acceptance Notes
Support artifacts are complete only when:
- customer help paths are predictable
- ticket and live-chat rules are not left informal
- data visibility is bounded responsibly
